How Sound Travels

Discover how sound moves through different materials!

🌊 Sound Needs Something to Travel Through

Remember: sound is made when things vibrate. But how does it get to your ears?

Sound travels in waves. But sound waves can't travel through empty space - they need something to move through!

💡
Big Idea: Sound travels through air, water, and solid objects. It travels at different speeds in different materials!
💨

Air

Sound travels through air. That's how you hear people talking!

🌊

Water

Sound travels through water. Whales and dolphins talk underwater!

🪵

Solids

Sound travels through solid things like wood, metal, and walls!

🚫

Empty Space

Sound CANNOT travel in space - there's no air or anything there!

🏃‍♂️ Sound Travels at Different Speeds

Sound moves faster through some materials than others!

💨
Through Air
767 mph
🌊
Through Water
3,300 mph
🔩
Through Steel
13,000 mph
FASTEST!

Why? In solid objects like steel, the molecules are closer together. They can pass vibrations faster!

🔬 Cool Sound Facts!

✓

Put your ear on a table and tap it - you'll hear it louder! Sound travels well through wood.

✓

Whales can "talk" to each other from hundreds of miles away underwater!

✓

In outer space, no one can hear you scream - there's no air for sound to travel through!

✓

When you knock on a door, sound travels through the door faster than through the air around it!
